Bulgogi (Korean BBQ Beef)

‚è±Ô∏è 30 mins
üë• 4 servings
üî• Easy

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
‚Ä¢ 1 onion, sliced
‚Ä¢ 4 tbsp soy sauce
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp sugar
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp sesame oil
‚Ä¢ 4 garlic cloves, minced
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp ginger, grated
‚Ä¢ 2 green onions, chopped
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp sesame seeds
‚Ä¢ Black pepper to taste

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and black pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Add the sliced beef and onions to the marinade. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 20 minutes (or overnight for best results).
  3. Heat a large skillet or grill pan over high heat. Add the marinated beef and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the beef is cooked through and caramelized, add green onions and cook for another minute.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve hot with steamed rice and kimchi.
üí° Pro Tip: For the most tender bulgogi, freeze the beef for 30 minutes before slicing. This makes it easier to cut paper-thin slices!

Kimchi Fried Rice

‚è±Ô∏è 15 mins
üë• 2 servings
üî• Very Easy

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 2 cups cooked rice (day-old works best)
‚Ä¢ 1 cup kimchi, chopped
‚Ä¢ 100g pork belly or bacon, diced
‚Ä¢ 2 eggs
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp kimchi juice
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp vegetable oil
‚Ä¢ 1 green onion, chopped
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp sesame oil
‚Ä¢ Roasted seaweed for garnish

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the pork belly until crispy.
  2. Add chopped kimchi and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until slightly caramelized.
  3. Add the rice, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add kimchi juice, gochugaru, and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  5. Push the rice to the side and fry eggs sunny-side up in the same pan.
  6. Drizzle with sesame oil, garnish with green onions and seaweed. Top with fried eggs and serve immediately.
üí° Pro Tip: Use day-old rice for the best texture. Fresh rice can become mushy when fried!

Bibimbap (Mixed Rice Bowl)

‚è±Ô∏è 45 mins
üë• 4 servings
üî• Medium

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 4 cups cooked rice
‚Ä¢ 200g beef, thinly sliced
‚Ä¢ 1 carrot, julienned
‚Ä¢ 1 zucchini, julienned
‚Ä¢ 100g spinach
‚Ä¢ 100g bean sprouts
‚Ä¢ 4 eggs
‚Ä¢ 4 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp sesame oil
‚Ä¢ Sesame seeds & seaweed for garnish

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. Marinate beef with 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, and minced garlic. Set aside.
  2. Blanch spinach and bean sprouts separately in boiling water for 2 minutes. Drain and season with salt and sesame oil.
  3. Saut√© carrot and zucchini separately with a pinch of salt until tender.
  4. Cook marinated beef in a hot pan until done. Fry eggs sunny-side up.
  5. In bowls, place hot rice at the bottom. Arrange all vegetables and beef in sections on top.
  6. Place a fried egg in the center. Add a dollop of gochujang, drizzle with sesame oil, and sprinkle sesame seeds.
  7. Mix everything together before eating. Enjoy!
üí° Pro Tip: Use a hot stone bowl (dolsot) if you have one for crispy rice at the bottom!

Korean Fried Chicken

‚è±Ô∏è 1 hour
üë• 4 servings
üî• Medium

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 1kg chicken wings or drumsticks
‚Ä¢ 1 cup potato starch or cornstarch
‚Ä¢ 4 tbsp gochujang
‚Ä¢ 3 tbsp honey
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp soy sauce
‚Ä¢ 3 garlic cloves, minced
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp rice vinegar
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp sesame seeds
‚Ä¢ Oil for deep frying
‚Ä¢ Salt & pepper to taste

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Let it sit for 15 minutes.
  2. Coat chicken pieces thoroughly with potato starch.
  3. Heat oil to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Fry chicken for 10-12 minutes until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  4. In a saucepan, mix gochujang, honey, soy sauce, garlic, and vinegar. Simmer for 3 minutes until thickened.
  5. Toss the fried chicken in the sauce until evenly coated.
  6. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve immediately with pickled radish.
üí° Pro Tip: Double-fry your chicken! Fry once, rest for 5 minutes, then fry again for extra crispiness!

Japchae (Stir-Fried Glass Noodles)

‚è±Ô∏è 40 mins
üë• 4 servings
üî• Medium

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 200g sweet potato glass noodles
‚Ä¢ 200g beef, thinly sliced
‚Ä¢ 1 carrot, julienned
‚Ä¢ 1 onion, sliced
‚Ä¢ 100g spinach
‚Ä¢ 5 shiitake mushrooms, sliced
‚Ä¢ 4 tbsp soy sauce
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp sugar
‚Ä¢ 3 tbsp sesame oil
‚Ä¢ Sesame seeds for garnish

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. Boil glass noodles for 6-7 minutes until tender. Drain and cut into shorter lengths. Toss with 1 tbsp sesame oil.
  2. Marinate beef with 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp sugar for 10 minutes.
  3. Blanch spinach in boiling water for 1 minute. Drain and season with salt and sesame oil.
  4. Stir-fry each vegetable separately (carrots, onions, mushrooms) with a bit of oil and salt. Set aside.
  5. Cook marinated beef until done.
  6. In a large pan, combine noodles, all vegetables, and beef. Add remaining soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil. Toss well over medium heat for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve warm or at room temperature.
üí° Pro Tip: Don't overcook the noodles! They should be chewy and translucent, not mushy.

Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cakes)

‚è±Ô∏è 25 mins
üë• 3 servings
üî• Easy

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 500g Korean rice cakes (tteok)
‚Ä¢ 100g fish cakes, sliced
‚Ä¢ 2 green onions, chopped
‚Ä¢ 3 tbsp gochujang
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp gochugaru
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp sugar
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp soy sauce
‚Ä¢ 2 cups water or dashi stock
‚Ä¢ 2 garlic cloves, minced
‚Ä¢ 1 boiled egg & sesame seeds

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. If using frozen rice cakes, soak them in cold water for 10 minutes.
  2. In a large pan, combine water, gochujang, gochugaru, sugar, soy sauce, and garlic. Bring to a boil.
  3. Add rice cakes and fish cakes. Simmer over medium heat for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and rice cakes are soft.
  4. Add green onions and cook for another minute.
  5. Serve hot, topped with a boiled egg and sesame seeds.
üí° Pro Tip: Add mozzarella cheese on top and let it melt for a trendy "cheese tteokbokki" variation!

Kimchi Jjigae (Kimchi Stew)

‚è±Ô∏è 30 mins
üë• 4 servings
üî• Easy

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 2 cups aged kimchi, chopped
‚Ä¢ 200g pork belly, sliced
‚Ä¢ 1 block firm tofu, cubed
‚Ä¢ 1 onion, sliced
‚Ä¢ 2 green onions, chopped
‚Ä¢ 3 cups water or anchovy stock
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp gochugaru
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp gochujang
‚Ä¢ 2 garlic cloves, minced
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp fish sauce

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. In a pot, cook pork belly over medium heat until it releases some fat.
  2. Add kimchi and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until slightly caramelized.
  3. Add water, gochugaru, gochujang, garlic, and fish sauce. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Add tofu and onion. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Add green onions and cook for 1 minute. Serve hot with steamed rice.
üí° Pro Tip: Use well-fermented, sour kimchi for the best depth of flavor!

Pajeon (Korean Scallion Pancakes)

‚è±Ô∏è 20 mins
üë• 2 servings
üî• Easy

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 1 cup all-purpose flour
‚Ä¢ 1 egg
‚Ä¢ 1 cup cold water
‚Ä¢ 1 bunch green onions, cut into 3-inch pieces
‚Ä¢ 1/2 red chili, sliced
‚Ä¢ 100g seafood mix (optional)
‚Ä¢ 3 tbsp vegetable oil
‚Ä¢ Salt to taste
‚Ä¢ Soy sauce & vinegar for dipping
‚Ä¢ 1 tsp sesame seeds

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, egg, cold water, and a pinch of salt to make a thin batter.
  2. Arrange green onions in a single layer in the batter. Add red chili and seafood if using.
  3. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large non-stick pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Pour the batter into the pan, spreading it evenly. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the bottom is golden and crispy.
  5. Flip the pancake carefully and add more oil around the edges. Cook for another 3 minutes.
  6. Cut into pieces and serve hot with soy-vinegar dipping sauce.
üí° Pro Tip: The key to crispy pajeon is using very cold water in the batter and plenty of oil while cooking!

Galbi (Korean BBQ Short Ribs)

‚è±Ô∏è 4 hours (with marinating)
üë• 4 servings
üî• Medium

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 1kg beef short ribs, cut flanken-style
‚Ä¢ 1/2 cup soy sauce
‚Ä¢ 1/4 cup brown sugar
‚Ä¢ 3 tbsp sesame oil
‚Ä¢ 6 garlic cloves, minced
‚Ä¢ 1 Asian pear, grated
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp mirin
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp ginger, grated
‚Ä¢ 2 green onions, chopped
‚Ä¢ Black pepper & sesame seeds

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, grated pear, mirin, ginger, and green onions to make the marinade.
  2. Add short ribs to the marinade, ensuring all pieces are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 3 hours (overnight is best).
  3. Preheat grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the ribs for 3-4 minutes per side until caramelized and cooked through.
  5. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and black pepper. Serve with lettuce wraps, ssamjang, and rice.
üí° Pro Tip: Asian pear is essential! It tenderizes the meat and adds natural sweetness to the marinade.

Jjajangmyeon (Black Bean Noodles)

‚è±Ô∏è 35 mins
üë• 4 servings
üî• Medium

üõí Ingredients

‚Ä¢ 400g fresh or dried jjajangmyeon noodles
‚Ä¢ 200g pork belly, diced
‚Ä¢ 1 onion, diced
‚Ä¢ 1 zucchini, diced
‚Ä¢ 1 potato, diced
‚Ä¢ 5 tbsp chunjang (black bean paste)
‚Ä¢ 2 tbsp vegetable oil
‚Ä¢ 2 cups water
‚Ä¢ 1 tbsp sugar
‚Ä¢ Cucumber slices for garnish

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water. Set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok. Add chunjang and fry for 2 minutes over low heat until fragrant. Set aside.
  3. In the same wok, heat remaining oil and cook pork until crispy.
  4. Add onion, potato, and zucchini. Stir-fry for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the fried chunjang, water, and sugar. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes until vegetables are tender and sauce thickens.
  6. Place noodles in bowls, pour the black bean sauce over them. Garnish with cucumber slices. Mix well before eating.
üí° Pro Tip: Frying the chunjang first removes the raw bean taste and enhances the flavor!
